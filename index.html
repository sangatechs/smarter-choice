What Is Domain Authority and How Is It Calculated?

DA is a predictive score that reflects how well a domain is likely to rank. Understanding it correctly changes how you approach link acquisition.

Domain authority is a metric developed to predict how well a website will rank in search engine results pages. It scores domains on a logarithmic scale from one to one hundred. Higher scores indicate stronger link profiles and, generally, better ranking potential across competitive queries. The metric is not a direct ranking factor used by search engines — but it correlates strongly with real-world performance because it reflects the same underlying signals algorithms actually evaluate.

SAAS PERSPECTIVE

Why Domain Authority Matters Differently for SaaS Brands

For SaaS companies, domain authority has a specific strategic dimension that differs from e-commerce or content-only sites. SaaS buyers research extensively before purchasing. They compare alternatives, read expert reviews, search for feature-specific terms, and look for social proof from trusted sources. Every touchpoint in that research journey represents an opportunity for links from relevant publications to influence both rankings and buyer perception simultaneously.

What SaaS-Specific Link Building Targets

Feature page rankings — links to specific functionality pages to rank for high-intent feature searches

Comparison pages — authority that helps your comparisons outrank competitor-created versions

Demo and trial pages — authority signals pushing conversion-focused pages higher in results

Integration pages — niche links from tools and platforms your software integrates with

Category authority — links that establish your domain in your specific software category

B2B buyer intent — publications where decision-makers research tools before purchasing

RANKING FACTORS

DA Is a Proxy — These Are the Real Ranking Drivers

Topical Relevance

Links from sites in your exact niche carry more topical authority than high-DA sites with no connection to your topic. Search engines now evaluate context, not just scores.

Editorial Quality

Links placed within genuinely useful, well-written content pass more value than links dropped into thin or low-effort pages regardless of the host domain's DA.

Link Velocity

Consistent acquisition over time builds more durable authority than burst campaigns. Steady growth signals organic influence to search algorithms.

Deep Page Authority

Building links to feature pages, guides, and product pages — not just the homepage — distributes authority where it converts, not just where it looks impressive.

SaaS Strategy Note: The most effective SaaS link building targets publications where your buyers are already spending time — industry newsletters, software review platforms, technology blogs, and integration partner sites. Links from these sources deliver both authority and qualified referral traffic from people actively evaluating tools like yours.

A high domain authority score does not guarantee that every page on your site will rank well. Page-level authority, content quality, technical SEO, and topical relevance all play equal or greater roles in determining where individual pages appear in results. DA is the starting point of a conversation, not the conclusion.

How to Build Domain Authority Effectively

Target Relevance First

Links from publications your buyers actually read multiply in value over generic high-DA placements

Vary Anchor Text

Branded, URL, partial match, and generic anchors across your profile signals natural editorial linking

Link Deep Pages

Spread authority to feature, demo, and comparison pages — not only the homepage

Track Real Rankings

Keyword position changes matter more than DA score movement as a success indicator

For SaaS businesses, the ultimate measure of link building success is not a higher DA score but a measurable increase in organic traffic to pages that drive trial sign-ups, demo requests, and product conversions. Authority is the mechanism — growth is the goal. Every link you acquire should be evaluated against whether it brings your target buyer one step closer to discovering your product.
